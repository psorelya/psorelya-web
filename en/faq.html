Are your products suitable for skin in full crisis (redness, extreme tightness)?

Absolutely. This is precisely the reason Psorelya exists. Our dermo-botanical protocols are formulated without superfluous ingredients, without denaturing alcohols and without aggressive sulfates. Our absolute priority is to soothe immediate discomfort while supporting the skin barrier.

Expert note: If your skin is "raw", we always recommend performing a patch test on a small area (such as the inner elbow) 24 hours before full application.
02

Will your products cure my skin condition (eczema, psoriasis, etc.)?

No — and we encourage you to be wary of cosmetic brands that promise "miracle cures". Psorelya is not a medication. Our products are dermo-botanical tools designed to break the cycle of discomfort (the dryness that leads to scratching) and provide lasting comfort. We soothe the discomfort, not the disease. Our mission is to restore your skin barrier and give it the tools it needs to repair itself naturally.

03

Do you use fragrances or essential oils in your formulas?

You will never find synthetic fragrances in our products — they are the number one cause of skin reactions. Our formulas derive their natural scent from our botanical actives (butters, plant oils, extracts). When we use essential oils (such as true lavender or chamomile), they are dosed at extremely low concentrations (less than 1%) to ensure maximum tolerance, even for reactive skin.

04

How long before I notice a difference in my skin?

The soothing of tightness and the comforting effect are often immediate, thanks to our lipid-rich ingredients (such as ceramides or shea butter). However, to observe a true improvement in the suppleness and resilience of your skin barrier, the skin needs time to renew itself.

We recommend consistent use, following the protocol specific to each product, for at least 28 days (one complete cell cycle) to evaluate deeper results.
05

Can I use Psorelya products on my child?

Our products are formulated with the utmost gentleness using natural ingredients widely recognised for their safety. However, as the skin of infants and young children is particularly thin and specific, we always recommend consulting your paediatrician or pharmacist before introducing any new skincare product into their routine, and performing a tolerance test beforehand.
